Using Linear Transformations for Derivative Calculation

Since we can treat the derivative operation for polynomials as a Vector spaces, since the properties of linearity applies to the derivative operation i.e. $$ \frac{d}{dx} (f(x)+g(x)) = \frac{d}{dx} f(x) + \frac{d}{dx}g(x)$$ and we also know that $$ \frac{d}{dx}cf(x)=c\frac{d}{dx}, c \in R $$ So we can define the derivative as a linear transformation with the following matrix $$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & ..\\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 0 & .. \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 3 & ..\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & .. \\ :&:&:&: \end{bmatrix}_{3 \times 3} $$ This matrix will have the effect of shifting each coffecient to $x^{n-1}$ and multiplying it by $n$
